Road

With big wheels and skinny tires road bikes are built with the general purpose of speed and long distances on cement and asphalt roads. Several specializations of road bikes are being designed these days, but for simplicity's sake, we analyze 3 categories:

Performance/Race, Aero, Endurance

Mountain

Designed to be able to tackle the toughest territory, mountain bikes can be the most overwhelming of bikes to buy with the amount of customizations possible.

Rigid bikes, hard tails, and full suspension.

Triathlon/TT

As the name indicates, triathlon (typically called "tri") or TT bikes are designed for competing in triathlons. They are typically designed for riding on straight and flat roads. If you plan on riding with friends, it's generally recommended to get a standard road bike.

Endurance

Race bikes tend to sacrifice some of the comfort items that can be found on endurance bicycles for the pure sake of speed.

Rather than an upright, comfortable position, race bikes will put you in a lower, more aerodynamic position. In addition, the frames will be geared towards a stiffer, more responsive dynamic which can sacrifice road absorption (asphalt texture and bumps).

Lastly, the pure race bikes will be outfitted with any and all parts that contribute the a lighter bike, but without loss of power (stiffness). With the advancement of technology, "lower grade" materials are much closer in weight and performance than they were in the past.
